Nikhil Govula

I was born and raised in India. My family and I migrated to Australia in 2016. I’ve moved to 6 schools throughout my schooling in countries of India, Singapore and Australia. I am multilingual to English, Hindi and Telugu since I learnt these growing up. I would love to try and learn a new language like French, Chinese or Japanese! Some of my accomplishments include making a purpose-built quadcopter for the delivery of liquid manure for crops as part of a school project, successfully completing my Remote Pilot License (RePL Australia) to fly drones with special access to certain areas and limitations and building a precision heavy-lift quadcopter that can lift up to 5 kg of payload with a full carbon fibre body and a GPS module using custom-designed 3D printed components. Additionally, I’ve thoroughly learned and fully utilised tools and software such as BetaFlight, iNav Firmware for tasks such as calibration, setup and testing of the GPS module, motors, flight controller and the Electronic Speed Controller (ESC). In this economy-based commerce, my fascination towards IT is how IT influences our society and culture and strives to improve our quality of life. I would say that personally I’m fascinated the most by the concept of software and hardware sub-components coming together to create a new interactive interface.

Renee Lee

I would describe myself as somewhat of a noob in terms of my IT skills, so I enrolled myself in this course to change that! Post-VCE I studied floristry for 2 years at Melbourne Polytechnic and have since been working in the industry for the last 3. Although I love my job I miss studying and have become increasingly aware of how essential IT skills are in any workplace. To absolutely no one’s surprise my hobbies and interests include watching anime, gaming and adding to my ever-growing manga collection. There are a few different experiences that ignited my further interest in IT. Firstly, working for a range of small businesses, I realised how difficult so many of the programs and systems can be for the average business owner to navigate. I am interested to learn about ways to make these programs more user friendly for people without an IT background or learn more so that I can help teach people how to use these programs more effectively. Secondly, seeing my grandparents struggle using basic technology, yet being so determined to keep up inspires me to help create something more intuitive and pared back for older and less experienced users to enjoy without feeling frustrated and confused.

Arvee Joshua Villahermosa
